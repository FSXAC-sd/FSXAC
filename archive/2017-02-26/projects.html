Autonomous Motorized Claw

Objective: Using provided materials (aluminium, rubber bands, Arduino) to build an automatically triggering claw that can pick up various objects from cloth to soup cans.

Process: We ultilized "Collaborative Sketching" to generate 6 different ideas, two of which was decided to be promising for us. Further developments was done on CAD software Autodesk Inventor and drawings were produced.

We used aluminium sheets and hand tools to manufacture claw parts using flat patterns from the CAD drawings. Experiments and risk analysis were made to make sure the claw performs to expectations.

The first iteration prototype was built directly from handtools, it was crude and controlled manually by hand and strings. Though rough and non-functioning, it gave us an idea of how the claw should work, and what we need to work on next.

Two more prototype made from cardboard were produced. They were used to experiment with picking up objects. The performance was scored based on if it was able to pick up certain objects, how well it picked them up, and the risk involved. We noticed one of our design is particularly good with picking up small objects such as dice and small rocks. But it lacks performance picking up large volume objects as the claw had trouble opening wide enough.

APSC Adaptive Devices

Objective: Design and model a handheld device for a paralysed individual that helps him to do daily task such as opening a door, eating, or playing guitar.

Throughout this project, we learned how to reduce risk and cost during the design process by building physical prototypes and mock-ups. This is because the project is physically oriented, where the target user's comfort and convenience is our top priority.

Virtual models were then made on Autodesk Inventor and Solidworks and a technical report was written highlighting the challenges and experiences throughout the project.
